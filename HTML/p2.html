Information Systems

Data vs Information

Data
• Raw facts, figures, symbols or observations lacking meaning until processed.
• Can be qualitative or quantitative.

Information
• Processed and organized data provided with context.
• Contextualized data.

Information Systems

• A structured setup of people, technology, processes and data working together to accomplish tasks for ease of management.

Types of Information Systems

Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)
• Handles day-to-day operations, fast and accurate for large volumes of iterative data.

Management Information Systems (MIS)
• Organizes and summarizes data to support structured decision-making.

Decision Support Systems (DSS)
• Provides possibilities, outcomes and risks for decisions.

Executive Information Systems (EIS)
• Provides executives with access to internal and external information such as financial performance.

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)
• Gathers customer purchase history, feedback and preferences.

Knowledge Management Systems (KMS)
• Collects, organizes and shares organizational knowledge.

Analysis

Information systems are vital to modern organizations. They expedite tasks involving large volumes of data that would otherwise require significant manpower. To understand their function, one must distinguish between raw data and processed information.

Information systems come in several types, each aiding specific organizational needs—from TPS handling daily operations to KMS preserving institutional knowledge.
