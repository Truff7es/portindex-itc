Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is port.index a monetized site?
A: No, port.index is completely free and not monetized in any way. It is a purely academic portfolio
created for the sole intention of displaying my outputs in CIS1102N course and is not used for
advertising, marketing, or generating revenue.

Q: Who created this website?
A: In compliance with my Introduction to Computing course requirement, this website was created by John Paul Tautuan,
a student enrolled in the BSIT course of the University of San Carlos. It serves as a compilation of
activities and outputs throughout the semester.

Q: The style looks familiar. Did you use a template?
A: No templates were used in the creation of port.index. The design and structural elements
were inspired by the the warez/piracy subculture, but the ASCII
was a touch borrowed from CODEX, a well-known scene group. All coding and design work were done from
scratch to ensure originality.

Q: Can I use the content from this site for my own projects?
A: The content on port.index is intended for educational purposes only. While you may reference the
structure and design elements, please ensure to create your own original content and not directly
copy any materials from this site.

Q: Will port.index remain accessible after the course ends?
A: It depends, port.index will remain accessible even after the completion of the CIS1102N course. However,
please note that it may not be updated or maintained beyond the duration of the course.
